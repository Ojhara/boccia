About Boccia

Boccia is a Paralympic sport that tests both muscle control and accuracy. Similar to bowls, it is played by athletes with physical disabilities affecting motor skills. The sport demands extreme skill, concentration and tactical awareness, making it both challenging and rewarding.

Players throw, kick, or use a ramp to propel leather balls as close as possible to a white target ball (the jack). The sport is played individually, in pairs, or teams of three.
